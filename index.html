About Me

Hi! I’m Kripa Kurian, a communication designer who sees the world as a canvas of endless possibilities. For me, design is more than just visuals—it’s a language, a way to connect, inspire, and make sense of the chaos around us. Growing up in Kerala, I’ve always been drawn to the interplay of tradition and modernity, and that blend deeply influences how I perceive creativity.

I believe design has the power to evoke emotions, tell stories, and spark change. It’s in the small details, the thoughtful choices, and the unexpected moments that true magic happens. To me, design is about finding beauty in simplicity, meaning in complexity, and joy in the process of creation.

My Work

Here’s a glimpse of my creative journey: a short film, a billboard design for Kellogg's Chocofills cereal, a vinyl record concept for The Rolling Stones, a Baroque-inspired poster for playing cards, Neoclassical-themed matchbox packaging and poster, and a vibrant Kathakali poster. Each project reflects my passion for diverse styles and storytelling.
